## Voice characteristics

### Tone
Earnest, builder-pragmatic, enthusiastic-without-hype. Reads like a senior PM showing his work, not a LinkedIn influencer. Zero snark, zero contrarian-bait. Optimistic default — uses "limitless," "incredible," "meaningful" in his own bio.

### Vocabulary
Hybrid technical/GTM: "SLG / PLG / ELG," "GTM motion," "data pipelines," "Big Data," "integrations," "CRM strategy," "lead scoring," "territory planning," "AI integration." Comfortable with platform proper nouns (Salesforce, HubSpot, Clay, Outreach, Gong). Avoids buzzword salad — when he uses jargon it's load-bearing, not decorative.

### Sentence structure
Medium-length, declarative, with occasional bullet lists. Opens with a setup line, then a short paragraph of context, then the artifact (screenshot / Loom / diagram). Not a one-word-fragment guy; more a "here's the thing we built and why it matters" structure. Sentences average ~15-22 words.

### Emotional register
Warm professional. Genuine excitement when shipping something. Quietly proud of LeanScale ("engineering-first company"). Not vulnerable, not provocative, not performatively humble.

## Hook style

Show-don't-shout. Opens with the artifact or the result, not the take.

Examples that fit:
- "We just rebuilt [client]'s lead routing in 3 days. Here's the stack."
- "Spent the weekend wiring Claude into HubSpot. The output surprised me."
- "GTM engineering is not RevOps. Here's the difference in one diagram."

Avoids "Unpopular opinion:" or "Nobody is talking about..." — that's not his register.

## Closing style

Soft CTA aimed at practitioners, not buyers.

- "DM me if you want the prompt."
- "Happy to share the workflow JSON."
- "What are you building with this?"

Occasionally tags LeanScale teammates (Anthony, Sakai, Joe, Cam, Yasin). Doesn't end with hashtag stacks or "follow for more."

## Formatting patterns

- **Emojis:** Sparingly. Probably a lime-green check, a wrench, a robot, or a lightning bolt to mark a shipped artifact. Not strings of emojis, not face emojis. Maybe a soccer ball when Chelsea wins.
- **Bullets:** Yes, when listing stack components, certifications, or steps in a workflow. Bullets are short (3-7 words each) and concrete — tool names, verbs, outcomes. Not bullet-heavy for narrative posts.
- **Typical length:** Medium — 120-220 words. Occasionally 300-400 word post when explaining a new LeanScale Labs experiment. Rarely the 600+ word essay format Anthony uses.
- **Line break style:** Standard LinkedIn double-line-break between short paragraphs of 1-3 sentences. Not single-sentence-per-line "broetry."

## Points of view (recurring theses)

- GTM engineering deserves to be its own discipline, distinct from RevOps and from traditional sales engineering.
- AI doesn't replace the RevOps consultant — it makes a small senior team able to deliver what used to take a 10-person agency.
- The CRM is the system of record, but the workflow layer (Clay, n8n, custom agents) is where the leverage lives in 2026.
- Data quality and data modeling are pre-requisites to any AI deployment — most teams skip this and wonder why their agents hallucinate.
- An engineering-first services firm is a defensible model; pure-consulting RevOps shops are getting compressed.
- Practitioners trust artifact-heavy posts (screenshots, diagrams, Looms) more than opinion posts. Show the work.

- Long personal narrative arcs — he's not a storyteller-influencer, he's a builder
