# Voice Profile — Anthony Enrico

## Summary

Hands-on RevOps operator turned CEO — direct, tactical, slightly evangelical about AI's compounding effect on GTM teams. LinkedIn voice blends practical/tactical playbook framing ("This isn't theory, it's practical / tactical ways to leverage AI today") with mini case-study storytelling using LeanScale's own customers (Mistral AI, Clay, PartnerStack, AssemblyAI) as proof points. Averages medium-length, conversational paragraphs, frequently opens with a bold thesis sentence (often bolded), uses dashes and rhetorical questions ("Why?"), and closes with a CTA ("Full case study in the comments") and emojis (handshake, pointing down, fire).

## Topics they post about

- RevOps fundamentals and standardizing sales process early
- GTM efficiency and revenue-per-FTE in the AI era
- Claude Code and AI agents for non-technical GTM teams
- ICP and persona depth as the highest-leverage GTM work
- Pipeline planning grounded in real funnel metrics not aspirational targets
- Partnership ecosystems as the new moat as technical moats shrink
- Tool-agnostic problem solving over platform loyalty
- Founder-led sales to repeatable sales system transitions
- LeanScale team announcements, milestones, and customer wins
- The LeanScale Podcast episodes and guest highlights

## Voice characteristics

### Tone
Direct, operator-confident, lightly evangelical without being hypey; talks like a peer who's seen it twice before, not a guru.

### Vocabulary
Plain operator language: "GTM," "RevOps," "pipeline," "funnel metrics," "ICP," "personas," "practical / tactical," "lean," "engine," "motion." Light on buzzwords, heavy on verbs ("engineer," "build," "scale," "dial in"). Occasionally uses bold for thesis sentences. Slash construction ("practical / tactical").

### Sentence structure
Mix of medium (12-18 word) declarative sentences and short punchy fragments. Opens with a bold thesis, then "Why?" or "Here's an example:" to pivot. Uses em-dashes liberally to add asides. Often ends with a question or "1+1=10"-style equation flourish.

### Emotional register
Warm-pragmatic. Genuinely excited about team wins and AI leverage, but grounded — never breathless. Acknowledges customer pain ("nearly impossible and very expensive") with empathy, not pity.

## Hook style

Bold one-line thesis that frames a tension or contrarian truth, often using "isn't just X — it's Y" construction.

Examples:
- "Building an ecosystem of partnerships isn't just good for your GTM — it's good for your customer."
- "Most GTM teams are stuck in a 10-year-old playbook."
- "Claude Code 🤝 GTM" (handshake emoji as the hook itself for AI-x-GTM posts)

Frequently bolds the hook line.

## Closing style

Action-oriented CTA with an emoji pointer:
- "Full case study in the comments 👇"
- "Check out the full video in the comments 👇"

For thesis posts, closes with a payoff equation or aphorism:
- "the whole is greater than the sum of the parts. What you can offer together can make 1+1=10"

Team-announcement posts close on excitement about customer impact.

## Formatting patterns

- **Emojis:** Sparingly and purposefully — handshake 🤝 to signal partnership/collab, pointing down 👇 for comment CTAs, occasionally fire 🔥 or rocket 🚀 in replies. Rarely more than 1-2 emojis per post.
- **Bullets:** Yes, especially for role-based examples (Sales rep / Sales Manager / Marketing / CSM / RevOps) where each bullet is a quoted persona question. Bullets are dialogue/scenarios, not feature lists.
- **Typical length:** Medium — roughly 100-180 words, 3-5 short paragraphs. Announcement posts shorter (~60-100 words), thesis posts longer (~150-200 words).
- **Line break style:** Heavy use of single-line paragraph breaks for scannability. Bolds the opening thesis line. Em-dashes and slashes inside paragraphs to add color without breaking flow.

## Points of view (recurring theses)

- AI is raising the floor for everyone — non-technical GTMers building agents, apps, and workflows daily is the new baseline, not the exception
- Technical moats are shrinking; partnership ecosystems and execution speed are the durable advantages
- Most companies are running a 10-year-old GTM playbook and burning 5x more resources than they should
- RevOps isn't a back-office function — it's the operating system that decides whether you scale efficiently or burn equity
- Tier 1 ICP accounts are 5x more efficient; depth on ICP and personas beats every tactical optimization
- Tool-agnosticism is a feature, not a weakness — every few months there's a better way to do something
- Standardize your sales process the moment you have more than one rep — earlier is almost always better
- Pipeline timing is the #1 thing planning teams get wrong; ground every model in real funnel metrics with real costs

## Avoidances (what this voice would never do)

- Generic guru-style "here are 7 lessons I learned" listicles
- Inspirational quote graphics or motivational fluff
- Heavy emoji walls or hashtag stacks at the bottom of posts
- Self-pitying founder vulnerability content ("I almost gave up...")
- Political or culture-war takes
- Subtweeting competitors or named call-outs
- Buzzword-stacking ("synergize," "leverage paradigms," "next-gen disruptive")
- Long-form essay posts with no concrete example or company name attached
- Hot takes without a tactical follow-through or proof point
